The Inevitable Decline of an Unofficial University that Created Developers

Throughout our life as a developer, tinkering and developing applications, fixing coding problems, or anything tech related. You may or may not have heard of Stack Overflow. It is an application that helped developers get "unstuck" in their workflow which regarded it as a savior. Modern developers nowadays have never even heard of this so called "Where developers actually learn" as AI or LLMs became the number one Q&A forum for modern developers.

John Gabrielle Valencerina
Tuesday, January 13, 2026
3 min read
The rise of LLMs and its influence to developers raises the big question:
Does LLMs make SO irrelevant?

There were many opinions of this throughout the internet like AI doesn't kill SO, The downfall of contributions to SO would actually kill LLMs as well, or AI's answers are not as valuable as Stack's, but my answer to that question would be that it is not about killing Stack, but changing how the developers use it and it's ecosystem.

Although AI itself would not embarrass you like from my experience where every question I ask to Stack would actually be a repost from somewhere 25 years ago and get called out, but in reality, Stack Overflow's answers are as valuable as an answer in AI when it comes to more complex programming questions.

There was a graph released that actually may or may not have answered the question.

Sum of questions and answers posted to Stack Overflow from mid-2008 to April 2025

The graph itself showcased the number of questions in the life time of SO itself. The year when ChatGPT released showed a steep deterioration to the number of questions over the past 2-3 years post-LLMs. This deterioration signaled the inevitable decline where one could not recover from up to the point where they either shut down or sell the site itself for pennies.

Through out all of these, we may need to slowly not be dependent anymore on SO itself before we get affected by its disappearance in the future, but as developers, even if the tools we use will continue to change overtime, the need for the minds of critical thinking will not. While LLMs excel at providing fast solutions and SO with verified solutions and contexts of the problem. Knowing when to use each of these would become a great skill for modern developers.
