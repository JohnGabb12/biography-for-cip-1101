Ink of My Own Story

By John Gabrielle Valencerina
Childhood Years | Teenage Years | College Years

Childhood Years

1My journey growing up began with an interest for drawing. 2I was drawing using those ballpens my father uses in his job. 3As soon as they noticed this, crayons and pencils were in my hand in the following moment. 4Each day, pencils and crayons were my toys which unlocked stories in my imagination. 5Before I could learn to read and count, boats in the sky, cars in the mountains, upside down waterfalls, and weirdly shaped landscapes were my language. 6Due to this, I was never the loud or outgoing child in my class. 7I preferred to stay in corners quietly shaping my next universe without distractions. 8Before you say I am a loner, I have friends along with me. 9I still remember the day where I saw that the classroom wall lacked something to which I drew and got scolded. 10It was one of the moments I realized how much I wanted to create. 11Around that time I started wearing glasses and made the world look sharper and more detailed than ever. 12Even those little things; the texture of a tree bark, the way shadows are formed, the waves of the waters, or the grass leaves of the plains, made my drawing a bit more detailed. 13Every new ideas and observations were on my paper the next second. 14Over time, I realized that for me, art was never just a hobby of some sorts, but also a way to communicate my ideas, my imagination, feelings if I had one, and thoughts. 15My sketches became more detailed and more colorful the way how I saw my world. 16These early experiences gave me confidence in my creative skills and help me understand who I was and what I loved to do. 17Even as a child, drawing and painting is where I felt the connection to. 18This connection guided my path throughout my childhood, at present, and towards the future. 19From those crayons and pencils which I first held, and the ballpen that led me to development, my passion for creating shaped my childhood as a whole. 20It became a part of who I am.

Teenage Years

1My teenage years were a time of growth and change in my life. 2I became close to a small group of friends who are also artistic and enjoyed drawing as much as I did. 3We shared sketches and stories during breaks, filled up sketchbooks, modeled clays, painted canvases, and drew on whiteboards. 4Although these friendships helped my creativity grow, these things distracted me from my schoolwork. 5Time after time, teachers reminded me to focus on my studies, but my attention always goes back to my drawings. 6Over time, our group of artist friends split up due to reasons, but my passion grew stronger than ever. 7Even quarantine did not stop me from communicating with my friends. 8Discovering video games became an important turning point for me, not because of addiction, but because on how artwork could come to life through those movement and interactions. 9I became curious on how these digital worlds were made and how those artistic visuals change. 10It lead me to programming which felt like discovering a new tool like those pencil and crayons during my first journey. 11With programming, I could take ideas that had stayed in papers and bring them to life on screen. 12I spent countless hours trying to figure out how things work, fixing mistakes, and eventually completing a game, although unrefined and cluttered, but it is a game which I am very proud of. 13Even though I am still struggling in some of my classes during that time, I found my self in pleasure in combining creativity and problem solving. 14Technology and storytelling became my passion with each stronger than the other. 15I see visions of the future where I can make my own interactive worlds, share stories beyond just the paper. 16My years during high school taught me the importance of patience and perseverance as learning new skills was never easy. 17There were times where I found myself stuck in a problem and thought that was it, but learned to grow and overcome these struggles. 18There was no AI to talk to so most of the time, forums after forums, documentation after documentation, or tutorials after tutorials, each leading me to find the answer to my problems. 19Creativity became a part of my identity, one that I am willing to nurture and protect. 20Each line in a sketch, each variable in a code, and each idea in my imagination contributed to the direction I would eventually take in life.

College Years

1My college experience was unlike what I expected. 2Unlike other colleagues who lived their lives in this country, for me it was a rollercoaster ride of an experience. 3Adjusting took me longer than I expected, well not because of the language, but because on how lively the streets are, the darker side of humanity, and how the people live their life in this country. 4These experiences halted my creativity as survival was the first and foremost importance at the moment. 5As I adjust to the new environment and my classes, my passion for drawing and technology goes back to me and I started creating more and more games side by side to my classes. 6College gave me the chance to learn more about digital technologies, software development, new languages, and visual arts in a supportive environment. 7Throughout my classes, group projects helped me learn how to collaborate with others and appreciate more ideas. 8Although the group of friends I have right now are not the artistic side, they are now a group of friends that are on the technology side of things. 9I learned to appreciate this new form of collaboration as much as the quiet artistic moments of my past. 10Long nights spent debugging code, designing assets, reminded me that creativity can be many forms. 11Joining organizations to pursue my path as a programmer with creativity, challenging myself to competitions, learning to grow between techstacks are where I am right now. 12In the past where competitions among artists is where thrived, competitions in technologies are where I pursue with the same determination. be more resilient and patient along with the virtue of effort in the face of challenges. 16My experiences here have taught me that growth happens outside comfort zones. 17By balancing academics, personal projects, and collaborative work, I feel that I am going to a path towards becoming a better and more artistic future of myself. 18I am beginning to understand how to turn my ideas into results that others can also experience. 19Every success and failures teaches me new things like discipline, creativity, and adaptability. 20With each projects and each breakthrough, I feel motivated to continue pushing the boundaries of what I can create and accomplish both art and technology.
